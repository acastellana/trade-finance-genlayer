The Problem
Why does this exist?
  • ⚡ Cross-border invoices often change after they are issued. Payment dates move. Shipment amounts change. FX exposure must be recomputed.
  • 🔄 Today this is handled manually between exporters, importers and treasury teams — with no shared source of truth.
  • 📊 Different parties may reference different benchmarks, leading to disputes over the final settlement amount.

This system produces one shared settlement outcome that both parties can verify.

The Solution
What the system does
1
Create the invoice settlement contract on-chain with both parties' addresses, invoice amount, and due date.
2
Lock the FX benchmark used for settlement — sourced from a decentralised oracle network (GenLayer) with validator consensus.
3
Apply the result consistently when trade conditions change — rolls, partial shipments, cancellations all follow the same on-chain logic.

The contract records the final settlement amount and its benchmark source.

How GenLayer Fits
Why this infrastructure

GenLayer is a decentralised decision network. Validators independently compute real-world data inputs such as FX benchmarks. Consensus among validators produces a reproducible settlement result.

Exception Handling
Where InternetCourt fits

If parties disagree about the trade state — for example shipment evidence or fraud — InternetCourt can resolve the dispute on-chain. The normal settlement flow does not require it. InternetCourt is an orthogonal module: it activates only when both parties cannot agree, and its outcome can be fed back into the settlement contract to unlock or cancel the trade.

Scope
What this is not
  • ✕ A letter of credit
  • ✕ A bank payment rail
  • ✕ A trade document platform
