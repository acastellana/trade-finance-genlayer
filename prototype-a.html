The contract handles the parts that are easy to verify:

  • the invoice amount
  • the benchmark rate

If shipment timing becomes contested, GenLayer evaluates the evidence and returns the fact the contract needs in order to continue.

Trade Lifecycle

From invoice to settlement

Five steps. One contract.

1
Invoice created
The trade terms are registered on-chain. A Bolivian exporter invoices 150,000 BOB to a Peruvian importer.
2
Benchmark locked
GenLayer validators agree on the benchmark rate. The locked benchmark is 0.493 PEN / BOB.
3
Conditional settlement funded
The contract computes the settlement amount. 150,000 × 0.493 = 73,950 PEN.

The importer pre-funds conditional escrow in PEN. Release depends on shipment status.
4
Shipment checked
If shipment is uncontested, settlement proceeds normally.

If shipment timing is contested, GenLayer evaluates the shipment evidence and classifies the outcome.
5
Outcome applied
The contract applies the pre-agreed economic consequence: full release, partial adjustment, cancellation, or manual review.

System behavior

How the system behaves

Normal case
GenLayer provides the benchmark and the trade settles.
Disputed case
GenLayer evaluates the shipment evidence and returns the fact the contract needs in order to release, adjust, cancel, or freeze the settlement.
The benchmark path is easier.
The disputed-evidence path is the real boundary.
That is why GenLayer is necessary here.
