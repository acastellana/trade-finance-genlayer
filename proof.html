Economic Model

Conditional escrow with pre-agreed remedy schedule

Importer pre-funds 73,950 PEN into escrow. Release depends on shipment timing. The court classifies the delay; the contract applies the remedy.

GenLayer decides the fact. The contract decides the money.

Court classificationExporter receivesImporter receivesLogic
ON_TIME 73,950.00 PEN (100%) — Full release
LATE_1_4 73,580.25 PEN (99.5%) 369.75 PEN (0.5%) Late-shipment adjustment
LATE_5_6 73,210.50 PEN (99.0%) 739.50 PEN (1.0%) Late-shipment adjustment
LATE_7_8 72,840.75 PEN (98.5%) 1,109.25 PEN (1.5%) Late-shipment adjustment
VERY_LATE (>8 days) — 73,950.00 PEN (100%) Buyer cancellation
UNDETERMINED Held in escrow — 14-day arbitrator window. If no ruling, defaults to refund. Manual review

The court question asks GenLayer to classify the shipment crossing into one of these buckets based on the evidence. The settlement contract maps the classification to the payout split deterministically. GenLayer never decides the price or the payment amount.

Disputed Scenarios

Three parallel deployments — one per classification path

Each scenario contract was deployed with different evidence to produce a different GenLayer classification. They are not branches of one live trade. The contract maps each classification to a deterministic payout split.

Scenario A — On Time

ON_TIME → 100% RELEASED
Evidence Exporter's customs exit receipt showed crossing at 22:41, 78 minutes before deadline. Importer's border gate record showed 00:18, 18 minutes after.
GenLayer classification ON_TIME — customs exit receipt is the authoritative crossing document.
Contract outcome 73,950 PEN (100%) released to exporter.

Scenario B — 1–4 Days Late

LATE_1_4 → 99.5% RELEASED

GenLayer classification LATE_1_4 — pre-clearance stamp is not a crossing event. Gate record shows crossing four days late.
Contract outcome 73,580.25 PEN (99.5%) to exporter. 369.75 PEN (0.5%) adjustment returned to importer.

Trust Model

Two delivery paths, two trust levels

FX Rate Delivery

Relayer calls receiveRate() directly on Base. Protected by onlyRelayer modifier.

The rate is a public market number — verifiable by anyone. No cryptographic bridge needed.

Verdict Delivery

GenLayer oracle → BridgeSender → relay → zkSync BridgeForwarder → LayerZero V2 → Base BridgeReceiver → InternetCourtFactory → settlement contract.

Protected by onlyVerdictSource: only accepts from courtContract (Factory, called only by BridgeReceiver). Relayer cannot forge a verdict.

Rate is relayer-mediated (no LayerZero). Classifications are bridge-mediated (through LayerZero). The asymmetry is intentional: wrong rate is publicly catchable; wrong classification triggers the wrong remedy. GenLayer classifies the fact; the contract applies the pre-agreed economic consequence.
